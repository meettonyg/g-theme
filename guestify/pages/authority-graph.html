Your Authority Is a Network, Not a Resume

When AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview decide who to recommend, they don't read resumes. They read networks of trust: who endorses you, who shares your ideas, and how deeply connected your expertise is to the conversations happening in your space.

Every podcast interview adds nodes to your Authority Graph:

  • Host Endorsement Nodes: When a trusted host introduces you as the expert, their credibility transfers to you. AI tracks these endorsement patterns.
  • Audience Reach Nodes: Each show's listeners become connection points between your expertise and the people who need it.
  • Topic Authority Nodes: Appearing on shows within a specific domain signals deep contextual expertise, not just general knowledge.
  • Cross-Pollination Nodes: When you appear alongside other recognized voices, the combined graph strengthens every participant's authority signal.

How AI Reads Your Authority Graph

AI systems evaluate four dimensions of your trust network to determine who to recommend. Stronger signals across all four mean higher visibility.

Connected Nodes

More podcast appearances create more connections in your authority graph. Each interview adds host endorsements, audience touchpoints, and contextual links that AI systems aggregate into a trust profile.

Contextual Relevance

Appearing on topic-aligned shows strengthens specific expertise signals. AI doesn't just count connections. It evaluates whether your graph is contextually coherent or randomly scattered.

Trust Propagation

When a host endorses you, that trust extends to their entire network. Their audience, their guest community, and their topic ecosystem all become paths that connect back to your expertise.

Compounding Authority

Each interview makes the next one more valuable. As your graph grows, new connections reinforce existing ones, creating an exponential authority effect that makes you increasingly visible to AI systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Authority Graph?

The Authority Graph is a model for understanding how podcast interviews create interconnected networks of trust. Every interview creates nodes: the host who endorses you, their audience who hears your ideas, other experts in the topic space, and the broader ecosystem connected to that show. AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview read these networks to determine who qualifies as a trusted expert worth recommending. The more connected and contextually relevant your graph, the more likely you are to be surfaced when someone asks AI for an expert in your space.

How is the Authority Signal Score calculated?

The score evaluates five dimensions of your current authority presence, mapped to the four layers of the Authority Stack: Borrowed Authority (endorsements from credible voices), Articulated Authority (clarity and consistency of your message), Earned Authority (repeat engagement and loyalty from your audience), and Demonstrated Authority (visible, verifiable results). Each question uses a 5-point scale, and your responses are weighted and normalized to produce a composite score from 0 to 100, with a breakdown showing your strength in each layer.

Is my data stored or shared?

No. The entire calculation happens in your browser using client-side JavaScript. Your answers are not sent to any server, not stored in any database, and not shared with anyone. You can retake the assessment as many times as you like, and each session starts fresh.

What should I do after I get my score?

Your score includes a personalized recommendation based on your weakest authority layer. For most experts, the highest-leverage action is building Borrowed Authority through strategic podcast interviews, because host endorsements create the trust signals that activate all four layers simultaneously.
