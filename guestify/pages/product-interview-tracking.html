How Does Interview Tracking Work?

Interview Tracking manages the full interview lifecycle in three phases, from pre-show preparation through post-interview outcome measurement and authority analysis.

1

Prepare With a Pre-Interview Brief

Before every interview, the system generates a preparation brief that includes the show's audience profile, the host's interview style, your positioning angle for this show, key talking points, and strategic goals for the appearance. You walk into every interview knowing exactly what success looks like.

2

Track the Interview Lifecycle

From recorded to edited to published, the pipeline tracks every interview's status. Log episode details when they go live: publish date, episode URL, show notes, and any assets the host created. The system prompts you for post-interview actions: thank-you notes, social shares, and content repurposing opportunities.

3

Measure What Mattered

Track the outcomes that authority-building interviews actually produce: website referral traffic, lead attribution, partnership introductions, speaking invitations, and follow-on opportunities. Over time, pattern recognition shows which show types, topics, and positioning angles deliver the strongest authority returns, so you can double down on what compounds.

Interview Pipeline

See every active interview and its status in a visual pipeline built for podcast guesting workflows. Columns map the real interview lifecycle: Booked → Prepared → Recorded → Published → Measured. Each card shows the show, host, scheduled date, and current status. Drag interviews between stages as they progress, add notes at each transition, and never lose track of where things stand across multiple active shows.

Outcome Tracking

Connect interviews to the outcomes that actually matter for authority building. Track referral traffic from episode links, attribute leads and inquiries to specific appearances, log relationship introductions from hosts, and record follow-on opportunities (speaking invitations, collaboration offers, media mentions). This is how you move from "I did a lot of interviews" to "I know exactly which interviews built my authority."

Authority Analytics

See how your Interview Authority compounds over time with analytics that measure what traditional metrics miss. Authority analytics tracks your growing network of host relationships, the breadth and depth of your audience reach, and the cumulative impact of your appearances on business outcomes. Pattern recognition surfaces which show types, topics, and positioning angles produce the strongest returns, so every future strategy decision is data-informed.

Interview Tracking Is Not For Everyone

This tool is built for experts who treat interviews as strategic investments worth measuring. It's not the right fit if you're looking for:

  • Download-count dashboards: If the only metric you care about is how many people played your episode, standard podcast analytics will suffice. Interview Tracking measures authority outcomes, not vanity metrics.
  • Social media scheduling: This isn't a content repurposing or social posting tool. It tracks what your interviews produced for your authority and business, not how many clips you posted.
  • Casual podcast appearances: If podcast interviews are a hobby rather than a strategic authority-building activity, the tracking depth will feel like overkill.
  • Instant ROI attribution: Authority compounds over time. If you need same-week conversion tracking, this isn't the right lens. Interview Tracking measures the outcomes that matter on authority timelines: weeks, months, and quarters.
